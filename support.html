What EchoField Does

EchoField helps you inspect your local network by discovering visible devices, showing available network details, and offering practical diagnostic information about your Wi-Fi or LAN environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why doesn’t the app show any devices?

Make sure your device is connected to Wi-Fi and that EchoField has permission to access your local network. Device discovery may also vary depending on your router, firewall rules, and what other devices expose on the network.

Why are some hostnames or device details missing?

Not all devices publish identifying information on a local network. The amount of detail available depends on the device itself, your router, and the network services available in your environment.

Why does EchoField ask for Local Network access?

EchoField needs Local Network permission to discover devices and inspect your LAN environment. Without it, core discovery and diagnostic features will not work.

Why does EchoField ask for Location access?

iOS may require location permission to allow access to certain Wi-Fi and network-related information. EchoField does not use location for tracking.

Does EchoField change my router or network settings?

No. EchoField is a read-only diagnostic utility. It does not modify your router, Wi-Fi configuration, or connected devices.

Does EchoField work in the iOS Simulator?

Some networking and Wi-Fi-related features may be limited or unavailable in the simulator. For best results, test on a physical iPhone or iPad connected to Wi-Fi.

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Confirm your device is connected to a Wi-Fi network
  • Check that Local Network permission is enabled
  • Restart the app after granting permissions
  • Try scanning again after reconnecting to Wi-Fi
  • Test on a physical device instead of the simulator
